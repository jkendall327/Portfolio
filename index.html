‘What kind of place are we, anyway?’

Here at Café INDIE, we’re proud to say that we’re the only non-profit co-op café in Scun-thorpe -- but what does that actually mean? How do you stay open if you don’t make any profit? And isn’t a ‘co-op’ one of those corner shops? Don’t worry: you’re not alone. While we’ve all heard these terms before, it can be hard to understand what they actually mean in practice. Let’s cut past the jargon and explain in clear terms how we’re different from your typical high street shop.The ‘non-profit’ part is the simpler of the two. All it means is that of the money we get from selling drinks, food or event tickets, we only keep what we need to keep the Café’s doors open. Most businesses see these things as necessary evils, where the real reward is the money you get over and above that—the profit. We, however, use that money to fund charitable causes, fill out our event schedule for the upcoming months or invest in the local community.The outcome is that we’ll be using our business to help improve Scunthorpe’s town center and put our work towards a good cause.

Now the trickier part—what it means to be a co-op-erative business. As the name suggests, the point is that we’re all in it together: everybody who works at the café has at least some say in how we run things. We even let customers buy a stake in the business, which lets them have their own influence on the direction we take go-ing forward. This applies to almost everything we do, from what food we serve to whether we offer alcohol, our opening times, and the events we put on.We don’t do this just for the sake of it, of course. Operating as a co-op helps us stay respons-ive to the needs of the community and our customers, since it stops any one of us from uni-laterally laying down how we think things should be. We’ve always been firm in our belief that we’re accepting of everyone who walks through our doors, and giving the public at large the real opportunity to make their voices heard seemed to us the only reasonable way of put-ting our money where our mouths were, so to speak.

Hopefully, this short post has gone some way to making those words feel a bit more natural on your tongue. That said, the best way to really understand what we’re all about is to come over and say hi in person—don’t worry, there’s no quiz—so we hope to see you in the café some time soon!
